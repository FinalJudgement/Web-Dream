Pay and Benefits

Web development can be a very satisfying career choice. One of the things that matters the most is our compensation. What is the pay like? And what are the benefits?

According to Occupational Outlook Handbook, in 2021 the median annual wage for a web developer was $77,450, with the lower 10 percent making around $38,550, and the upper 10 percent making around $168,200.

With that being said, some may be concerned about the 38,500 minimum salaries, but this isn't typical, and there could be many reasons why the wage is low. One of my favorite things about web development is the vast opportunities that workers have to improve and fill more senior positions, or the freedom to change to a company that suits them better.

It's difficult to quantify the benefits of being a web developer due to every company being different. However, there are some common benefits that we can see through web developer careers in general.

These Benefits include:

  • Working Remotely
  • High Potential for Creativity
  • Working and Traveling
  • Freelancing
  • Career Growth
  • Pay
  • Transferrable Skills(into other programming jobs)
  • No Degree Required

Also, if you happen to land a job at a big tech company, you could see much more benefits, including free meals, free commutes, and even free housing. One of the most important things to note is that employees that are web developers enjoy their freedoms and life styles. And if you enjoy your work, then you never feel like you're working at all. This is why I want to become a Web Developer.
